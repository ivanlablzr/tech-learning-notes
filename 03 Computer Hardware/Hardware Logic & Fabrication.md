How the abstract logic of computation becomes physical silicon — the bridge from [[Digital Logic & Microcontrollers|digital logic]] up to a manufactured chip.

## 1. Computational logic (recap)

The layers that turn switches into arithmetic (full treatment in [[Digital Logic & Microcontrollers]]):

- **Boolean algebra & truth tables** — the math of 0/1.
- **Combinational logic** — gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR) with no memory; output depends only on current inputs. Building blocks: adders, **multiplexers/demultiplexers**, decoders.
- **Sequential logic** — adds memory via flip-flops → **registers, counters** (see [[Memory#6. SRAM and the latch (how a bit is physically stored)|latches & flip-flops]]).
- **Binary arithmetic** — addition/subtraction (two's complement), multiplication, division, bitwise ops and shifts — what the [[CPU & Processing Units#1.1 Core building blocks|ALU]] actually executes.

## 2. Photolithography — printing the chip

Every microchip (CPU, GPU, SoC, DRAM, NAND) is patterned onto a silicon **wafer** by projecting light through a **photomask (reticle)** onto light-sensitive resist, etching nanoscopic transistor circuitry layer by layer.

**The leading-edge process — EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet):**
- EUV light (13.5 nm wavelength) isn't found in nature; it's generated by blasting tin droplets with a laser to create plasma.
- The light is steered by **field-facet and pupil-facet mirrors** (EUV is absorbed by lenses, so reflective optics are used) and shaped by illumination patterns (annular, dipole) onto the wafer.
- **DUV (Deep Ultraviolet)** machines handle older/larger nodes; **EUV** enables the smallest modern nodes.
- **ASML** (Netherlands) is the sole maker of EUV lithography machines — a strategic chokepoint in the global semiconductor supply chain.

> This is the literal bottom of the stack: physics + optics → transistors → [[Digital Logic & Microcontrollers|gates]] → [[CPU & Processing Units|processors]]. Control over fabrication (fabs, EUV tools, leading nodes) is now central to technology geopolitics.
