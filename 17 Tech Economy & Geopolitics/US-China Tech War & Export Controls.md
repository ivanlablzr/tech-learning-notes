# US-China Tech War & Export Controls

The defining geopolitical contest of the era: the US and China fighting for control of the technologies that confer economic and military power — above all **advanced chips and AI**. Because value and capability concentrate at a few [[The Technology Value Chain#6. Chokepoints — the few links that hold everyone hostage|chokepoints]], whoever controls those chokepoints can throttle a rival's entire industry. This note covers the weapons, the moves, and the countermoves.

## 1. Why chips are the battlefield

Advanced semiconductors are **dual-use** — the same GPUs that train ChatGPT train weapons-targeting and surveillance AI. The US holds (with allies) the chip **chokepoints**: chip *design* + EDA tools (US), the GPU + CUDA stack (**Nvidia**), and — critically — **ASML's EUV monopoly** (Netherlands) and **TSMC's** leading-edge fabrication (Taiwan). The US strategy, articulated as **"small yard, high fence"**, is to deny China the *most advanced* compute while leaving everyday chips alone — a **"sliding scale"** updated as China catches up.

## 2. The US export-control toolkit

| Instrument | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Entity List** | bars named firms from buying US tech without a license (Huawei 2019; SMIC 2020; **~65 more Chinese entities added in 2025**) |
| **Foreign Direct Product Rule (FDPR)** | extends US jurisdiction to *foreign-made* goods that used **any** US tech/software — the long-arm weapon that cut Huawei off from TSMC |
| **Performance thresholds** | bans chips above compute/interconnect limits (caught the A100/H100; then the China-specific A800/H800; **then the H20**) |
| **Equipment controls** | with the Netherlands & Japan, blocks **EUV** (and now advanced DUV) lithography sales to China |
| **Country tiers** | a worldwide licensing scheme (Tier 1 allies → Tier 3 restricted) controlling where the best AI chips can go |

**The Nvidia case shows the bite:** chips were redesigned (A800/H800) to skirt limits, then re-banned; in **April 2025 the US required a license for even the cut-down H20**, costing Nvidia a **$4.5B inventory charge and ~$8B in lost China revenue**. (The Trump administration has since been **tightening China enforcement while relaxing Gulf-state** access.) H100/H200/Blackwell remain fully barred from China.

## 3. China's countermoves

China retaliates by weaponizing **its own chokepoints** and racing for self-sufficiency:

- **Mineral leverage** — China refines ~**70%** of critical minerals (gallium ~99%, rare earths ~91%). It imposed **gallium/germanium export controls (2023)**, then **expanded rare-earth controls (Oct 2025)** to magnet/defense elements — a direct squeeze on Western chip, EV, and weapons supply chains.
- **Indigenization** — **Made in China 2025** + huge "Big Fund" subsidies push self-reliance. **SMIC** reached 7nm (via DUV multi-patterning) and **Huawei** re-emerged with domestic AI accelerators (Ascend) — progress, but still **generations behind** at the leading edge and capacity-constrained without EUV.
- **Stockpiling & smuggling** — front-loaded GPU/equipment buying ahead of bans, plus grey-market diversion the controls struggle to stop.
- **Retaliatory pressure** — antitrust probes and procurement bans on US firms (Micron, Intel) operating in China.

## 4. Beyond chips — the broader decoupling

The contest spans the whole stack: the **Huawei 5G** bans across Five Eyes + allies (security + standards power); the long **TikTok** fight (data + algorithm control, forced-divestiture pressure); **outbound-investment screening** (US restricting capital into Chinese AI/quantum/chips) alongside inbound **CFIUS**; a **talent and research** decoupling (visa scrutiny, the end of open lab collaboration); and **submarine-cable** route-blocking (the FCC rejecting Pacific Light's HK landing — see [[Submarine Cables]], [[East-Asia Digital Infrastructure]]).

## 5. The Taiwan fulcrum

It all converges on Taiwan: **TSMC makes the world's most advanced chips on an island China claims**, making it a single point of failure for the global economy (a conflict/blockade is often modeled in the **trillions**). This is both the West's greatest vulnerability and, per the **"Silicon Shield"** thesis, a deterrent — and it's the engine behind the **reshoring** push (CHIPS Acts, TSMC Arizona) covered in [[Tech Sovereignty & Governance]].

## 6. Where it's heading

Likely a durable, partial **"decoupling" / "de-risking"** rather than a clean break: bifurcating standards and supply chains, an AI-compute gap the US tries to hold open, and China grinding toward self-sufficiency at the trailing-and-eventually-leading edge. The risk is escalation (broader controls ↔ broader mineral retaliation) tipping into a **fragmented "splinternet" of technology blocs** — the governance dimension of which is in [[Tech Sovereignty & Governance]].
