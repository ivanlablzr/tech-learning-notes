RIR: **APNIC**. Anchored by **Singapore** — the world's #2 cable hub and APAC's peering/cloud capital — and ranging from open city-states to some of Asia's most aggressive internet-centralization moves. (Part of [[Internet & Infrastructure]].)

## Singapore — the APAC hub

Penetration ~96%; regulator **IMDA**. At the junction of the Indian Ocean and South China Sea, **~25+ cable systems land here — second only to the USA**, controlling access through the **Strait of Malacca** (the main Asia–Europe cable corridor). It's APAC's **premier peering point** (SGIX/Equinix ~3 Tbps — the Amsterdam of Asia) and hosts **every major cloud** (AWS ap-southeast-1 since 2010, GCP, Azure, Alibaba, Tencent). **Singtel** (Temasek ~52%, a global cable operator that owns Optus) leads ISPs on a unique **structurally-separated fiber model** (NetLink Trust wholesales passive fiber to all retailers).

> A **2019–22 DC construction moratorium** (power limits) pushed builds across the causeway to **Johor, Malaysia** — now a booming hyperscale corridor (Google/Microsoft/AWS each >$2B committed). Governance is rule-of-law but features **POFMA** (government "falsehood" correction/removal orders).

## Vietnam — the military-telecom model

Penetration ~79%; **VNPT/MIC**. Dominated by **Viettel** (AS7552, owned by the **Ministry of Defence**) — a military telecom that is also **uniquely globally expansive, operating in 11 countries** (Cambodia/Metfone, Laos, Myanmar/Mytel, Mozambique, Peru, East Timor…). The state exercises tight control: a 2018 cybersecurity law mandates data localization, and **Facebook (the dominant platform) is throttled during politically sensitive periods**.

## The control escalators

- **Cambodia** (~62%): established a **National Internet Gateway (2021)** — a China-GFW-style single government-controlled chokepoint all ISPs must route through (full visibility + blocking); enforced by 2022, one of Asia's most dramatic centralizations. Viettel's Metfone is a top operator.
- **Myanmar** (~53%, declining): after the **Feb 2021 coup**, the junta imposed **weeks of nationwide shutdown** + months of nightly curfews and Facebook blocks; **Telenor and Ooredoo exited** rather than install junta-mandated backdoors (Telenor sold cheap to M1 Group). Military-linked **Mytel** (Viettel 49%) profits.
- **Laos** (~45%): state-dominated, China-aligned; **Brunei** (~95%): wealthy, state-run AITI; **Timor-Leste** (~35%): tiny, Viettel-served.
